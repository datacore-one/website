How Datacore learns what you care about

Built on decades of cognitive science and productivity research. No hallucinations, no guesswork — structured memory that sharpens with every session.

Standing on the shoulders of giants

  • GTD (David Allen) — Capture everything, clarify next actions, review weekly. The system that frees your mind from open loops.
  • Zettelkasten (Niklas Luhmann) — Atomic notes linked by meaning, not hierarchy. 70,000 notes became 70 books. Your knowledge connects the same way.
  • Spaced repetition — Memory research shows that knowledge decays without reinforcement. Engrams surface at the right moment, strengthening what matters.
  • Progressive summarization (Tiago Forte) — Layer highlights on highlights. Each pass distills signal from noise. Your literature notes work the same way.

How Datacore learns: Engrams

Every session, Datacore observes your decisions, preferences, and corrections. It distills them into engrams — structured memory units. Engrams are not chat history. They are compressed knowledge with activation thresholds, decay curves, and domain tags. The system sharpens itself over time.

Example engram

# Learned after 3 sessions
statement: "User prefers bullet points over
  paragraphs for research output"
type: behavioral
domain: research.formatting
activation: 0.89
source: 3 corrections across sessions
# Installed from community pack
statement: "When writing docs, lead with
  the outcome, not the process"
type: procedural
domain: writing.documentation
pack: technical-writing-v1

Engrams decay over time unless reinforced. High-activation engrams surface automatically. Low-activation ones are retired. The system sharpens itself.
